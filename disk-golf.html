Andy's Disk Golf Game - Unreal Engine 5

Project Summary

Developed a personalized disk golf game for Andy Zosel's 50th birthday. The unlockable characters are all members of Andy's family, and their unique skills reflect their real personalities!

  • Features: Frisbee physics, unlockable characters, quests/challenges, levels, stat tracking, savegame
  • Skills: Unreal Engine 5, MetaHuman, C++, Blueprints
View and download the project on GitHub

Unique Characters and Unlocking System

A key game system I developed was the characters and the system to unlock them. Designed in UE's MetaHuman creator, each character is an accurate representation of members of Andy's family. Additionally, each character has a unique set of skills that reflect their real personality and lead to unique gameplay.

The quests to unlock each character encourage varied gameplay. Their varied difficulty create rewards, challenge, and player progresssion. It also encourages use of different character skills to accomplish specific goals.

To unlock all characters, players must master the game's mechanics and become familiar with every map.

Level Design

An important feature for a fun disk golf game is the fun disk golf courses!

I designed a series of maps that challenge players in a variety of ways. I created obstacles like trees, rocks, structures, and water traps. Some levels test throwing accuracy, while others challenge platforming skills. Different character skills (speed, waterproof, projectile visualization) lead to unique gameplay within each level.

Frisbee Physics (C++)

Programmed a deterministic flight simulation in C++, including drag, lift, and pitching force. Players can learn and master the frisbee dynamics to improve their score on each course. Players can control tbe frisbee roll angle, power, spin direction, and overall trajectory. Some character skills create a trajectory visualization. Due to the deterministic nature, game includes a replay system for players to review their previous throws and understand the performance of the frisbee.

Physics simulation based on: Simulation of a spin stabilized sports disc, Crowther and Potts (2007)
